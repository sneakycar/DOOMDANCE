Susan was born in 1931. She never left her home town of Farmington. Some folks said she had no concept of reality. But Susan knew better. She loved mowing the lawn and going for long walks in the park. She never had to get stitches and she never learned to swim. Susan never got married and never had a child. She had 2 imaginary friends named Beverly and Jim, but they were nice people... they never told her to hurt anybody. Susan lived a good life and died in 1983 from brain cancer. They buried her in Memorial Gardens Cemetary, and no one visits her. One boy and girl put a lilac by her grave for Memorial Day, but they did not know who she was. They just felt sorry for her because her gravestone had no name on it.

Gerald... the most popular member of the family. Everyone liked him. He was such a unique personality. He could do anything he wished. He proved it, day in and day out. He offered words of wisdom to friends and strangers alike. He had not one single enemy.

Some folks called him Priestly - we think he was too smart for his own good. He mowed the lawn a lot, and he never wore shorts. His father was bitter (a firefighter). He hated chocolate and war, and he was afraid of fireworks and supermarkets. He had 2 arms, because having 2 arms is normal, and also, he worked at an ice cream parlor.

But then, July 27, 1967. The day Gerald went fishing. He left with such a smile on his face, eager to catch some walleye. He took off in his Chevy, and was never seen again. We've done all we can to locate our dearest son Gerald, but to no avail. It appears, Gerald may well never return.

He loved fishing and ice cream cones. The Beatles - Revolver was his favorite album, mostly due to his favorite song, Eleanor Rigby. He loved the Boston Red Sox and boy did he love that Chevy. He always said, "The man who lasts longest is the last man standing." Brilliant irony that only Gerald could give. We will forever miss Gerald, and pray that he is still alive. Perhaps he started a new life somewhere. Perhaps he was in a car accident and developed amnesia. The fact is, we will never know what happened to our dear son Gerald, but nothing could diminish our love for him. HEAR YOU ME GERALD.

The last known piece of information on Gerald is this journal entry, dated July 27, 1967. A beautiful poem he wrote, perhaps describing his relationship with highschool sweetheart Augusta.

"he grew up a good boy. he was well received. he lost his mind. he fell on the sidewalk. his cowboy boots did not fit. his head bleeds. his bicycle is broken."
